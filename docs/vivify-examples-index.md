# Vivify Examples — Index

Reference corpus of Vivify maps to study techniques, patterns, and gotchas already solved by the community. **Not committed** (`vivify_examples/` is in `.gitignore`): it's ~50MB of the mappers' IP, reproducible via beatsaver IDs.

## How to get the maps

```
1. Go to https://beatsaver.com/maps/<ID>
2. Download Zip
3. Unzip into my_vivify_template/vivify_examples/<folder>/
4. Optional: delete the .egg to save space (we don't need it for analysis)
```

### How to read the table

- **Env**: the vanilla BS environment the map uses as a base. Hint at what to build on top of.
- **Bundle (MB)**: size of the `bundleWindows2021.vivify`. `--` = the map doesn't use its own bundle (might only use Vivify for shaders/post-process without custom 3D).
- **CustomEvts**: total events in `_customData._customEvents` (summing all difficulties).
- **Diffs**: how many difficulty `.dat` files (not counting `Info.dat`/`BPMInfo.dat`).
- **EnvDisable**: whether they use `_customData.environment[]` with Chroma commands to hide/customize the vanilla environment.
- **Event types**: deduplicated list of `_type` from the `_customEvents`. If we see something we never use, it's worth understanding why.

## Observations (post-index 2026-05-02)

> Each observation is ONE concrete question to discuss in a new conversation, **not a recommendation**. The goal is to preserve the current approach and only adjust what, after thinking, is worth it — not converge on "do what they do" by default.

### What they consistently do that we don't

1. **`Blit` (10/10).** Universal across the corpus. It's the post-processing primitive (apply a shader to the frame's render output). We don't use Blit yet. **Question:** does most of Vivify's visual flair come through post-processing, and are we closing that door by going all-in on 3D prefabs? Would it make sense to add a Blit at key Phase 1 moments (successful parry, hit taken), or do we reserve it for Phase 2/3?

2. **`DestroyObject` (10/10).** Universal. Cleanup of instances. We don't — because today our only prefab persists for the whole map. **Question:** when the attack prefabs (projectiles, indicators) arrive, do we plan explicit cleanup or trust the map reload to pick them up? Risk of leak between runs if the player restarts from the menu.

3. **`AnimateTrack` (10/10).** Universal. We don't yet, even though the `vivify-mapping` skill lists it as a standard event. **Question:** are we solving things with `SetAnimatorProperty` that would be cleaner with `AnimateTrack` (point definitions, native easings)? Concrete case: when we position Aline in the cosmetic intro, `AnimateTrack` on the prefab's track vs setting Animator properties?

4. **`AssignObjectPrefab` (7/10).** Majority. Used to replace native BS objects (notes, sabers, debris) with custom prefabs. We don't use it. **Question:** for Phase 1 probably irrelevant (focus on Aline), but is there any moment in Phase 1 where customizing the notes (e.g. notes as "paint fragments" when Aline attacks) would add value without scope creep?

5. **`EnvDisable` via `_customData.environment[]` (10/10).** Universal. Confirms that the next step (hiding Timbaland) is standard, not exotic.

### What we do that none of them do

1. **`TimbalandEnvironment`.** Nobody in the corpus uses it. The distribution is: DefaultEnvironment ×3 (Aeroluna 42-flux, Chaimzy End Times, nasafrasa luminescent), BillieEnvironment ×2 (Swifter you, nasafrasa Lifelike), BigMirrorEnvironment ×2 (Droobix, Elecast), singletons RocketEnvironment (Sands), TriangleEnvironment (Jan Ilavsky), WeaveEnvironment (Mawntee). **Question:** Aeroluna and Swifter (the ones who know the stack best) pick Default and Billie respectively — probable signal that those are the cheapest to clean up. If we're going to hide Timbaland anyway, is it worth switching to one that starts cleaner? Cost: change in `Info.dat`, possible impact on how the vanilla lights behave (which we already don't care about with Vivify), benefit: less disable regex.

2. **Heavy bundle with its own 3D asset.** Only Breezer (Jan Ilavsky, 11MB) has a bundle in this corpus of 10. Most do flair with procedural primitives (Blit + Chroma env). Our bundle is 70MB because we cram in the whole Aline model + animations. **Question:** are we in a less-traveled niche of Vivify (character-driven vs effect-driven)? **Practical implication:** Breezer is the obvious deep-dive, the only useful corpus comparison point for our pattern.

3. **`SetAnimatorProperty` as main driver.** 2/10 use it (Aeroluna 42-flux, Elecast Yoi Okashi); for us it's our most recurring event. **Question:** is it because nobody does animated characters (when they do, e.g. Aeroluna, they do use it) or is there an alternative we're not seeing? Probably the former — but confirm by looking at 42-flux, which is the closest to "character + animator state machine".

### Curiosities / minor leads

- **`SetRenderSetting` (singular)** shows up in Elecast (Yoi Okashi). The correct one is `SetRenderingSettings` (plural). Probable typo by the author; BS would silently ignore it. **Not actionable** beyond the curiosity of "typos in `_type` are silent failures".
- **Custom event counts** vary enormously: Mawntee 130, Elecast 15570 (!). Ours will end up in the hundreds for Phase 1; the "normal" range looks like 1000-3000. Useful as a baseline to detect if we're overdoing it.
- **Only Aeroluna uses `SetGlobalProperty`** (along with nasafrasa-Lifelike and Mawntee). Niche for uniforms shared across materials. Not a priority for us.

### Open questions for a new conversation

1. Do we swap `TimbalandEnvironment` for `DefaultEnvironment` or `BillieEnvironment`? Cost vs benefit of disable.
2. Do we deep-dive Breezer (43a47) specifically, given it's the only one with a heavy bundle in the corpus?
3. Where does `Blit` fit into Phase 1, if at all? Potential cases: successful parry, player hit, cosmetic intro.
4. Are there current tasks we're solving with `SetAnimatorProperty` that would gain in cleanliness with `AnimateTrack`?
5. Do we need `DestroyObject` when the attack prefabs arrive, or do we trust the map reload?
6. Is it worth confirming Elecast's typo (`SetRenderSetting`) in the BS source to understand whether it's a real alias or a silent fail?
